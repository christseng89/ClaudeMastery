## 1. Xinyi District - Near Taipei 101 & Elephant Mountain {#xinyi-district}

### Din Tai Fung (鼎泰豐) - Taipei 101 Branch
**Location:** B1, No. 45 Shifu Road, Taipei 101 (Exit 4 of Taipei 101 MRT)
**Cuisine:** Taiwanese/Soup Dumplings
**Price Range:** $$ (NT$400-800 per person)
**Hours:** Varies, typically 11 AM - 9 PM

**Why It's Perfect:**
- World-famous xiaolongbao (soup dumplings) with 18 perfect folds
- Michelin recognition - ranked among world's top restaurants
- Perfect for first-time visitors to try Taiwan's signature dish
- Clean, modern atmosphere teens appreciate
- English menu available

**What to Order:**
- Pork xiaolongbao (必點!) - NT$110 for 5 pieces
- Truffle dumplings (upscale treat)
- Fried rice
- Shrimp and pork wontons
- Sweet and sour pork

**Pro Tips:**
- Expect 30-60 minute wait (longer on weekends) - no reservations accepted
- Visit Monday-Thursday or just outside peak lunch/dinner hours (2-5 PM)
- The Taipei 101 location is convenient but busy - consider Xinyi Road location for shorter waits
- Watch dumpling-making through glass windows
- Typical meal costs NT$1,400 for 2 people

**Practical:** Great location right after visiting Taipei 101 observatory or before/after Elephant Mountain hike

### Ay-Chung Flour-Rice Noodle (阿宗麵線)
**Location:** No. 8-1, Emei Street, Ximending
**Cuisine:** Taiwanese Traditional
**Price Range:** $ (NT$60-100)
**Famous Dish:** Oyster Vermicelli

**Why It's Perfect:**
- Ximending institution - been serving since 1975
- Just ONE dish on menu - Taiwanese-style vermicelli with braised pork intestine
- Standing-only eating (street food style)
- Quick, cheap, authentic experience
- Always crowded with locals - sign of quality

**What to Order:**
- Small or large bowl of mee sua (that's it!)
- Optional: Add extra vinegar and chili

**Pro Tips:**
- Stand on sidewalk and eat like locals
- Cash only
- Try it even if "intestines" sound scary - the flavor is incredible
- Takes 5 minutes to eat - perfect snack while shopping

## 3. Shilin Area - Near Night Market {#shilin-area}

### Shilin Night Market Food Recommendations

While Shilin Night Market is primarily street food, here are the MUST-TRY stalls:

#### Hot-Star Fried Chicken (豪大大雞排)
**Location:** Near market entrance
**Price:** $ (NT$90)
**Famous For:** Chicken cutlet literally BIGGER THAN YOUR FACE

**Why Teens Will Love It:**
- Massive size is impressive and Instagram-worthy
- Chicken marinated 24 hours for incredible flavor
- Crispy, juicy, and perfectly seasoned
- Sharable but teens might want their own
- Long queues = photo opportunity

**Pro Tips:**
- Expect 10-15 minute wait in queue (worth it!)
- Available in mild or spicy
- Use it as a meal, not just a snack
- Take size comparison photo before eating

---

#### Ay Hui Vermicelli (A Hui 蚵仔麵線)
**Location:** Inside Shilin Night Market
**Price:** $ (NT$50-80)
**Recognition:** Michelin Bib Gourmand award winner

**What It Is:**
- Oyster vermicelli noodles
- Thick, gooey consistency (might seem strange at first)
- Incredibly flavorful
- Authentic Taiwanese comfort food

---

#### Haiyou Shiquan Herbal Stewed Pork Ribs
**Location:** Shilin Night Market
**Price:** $ (NT$100-150)
**Recognition:** Michelin Bib Gourmand recipient

**What It Is:**
- Tender pork ribs in rich, flavorful herbal broth
- Perfectly cooked meat
- Traditional Taiwanese medicinal cuisine

---

#### Raohe Street Fuzhou Black Pepper Buns (Shilin Branch)
**Location:** Shilin Night Market
**Price:** $ (NT$60-80)
**Famous For:** Peppery pork buns roasted in clay oven

**Why It's Amazing:**
- Crispy on outside, juicy on inside
- Strong pepper flavor (teens who like spice will love it)

#### Prince Cheese Potato (王子起司馬鈴薯)
**Location:** Shilin Night Market

**What It Is:**
- Whole baked potato with bright orange cheese sauce

**Why Teens Like It:**

#### Taiwanese Sausage with Sticky Rice (大腸包小腸)

**What It Is:**
- Grilled sticky rice acts as the "bun"

## 7. Bubble Tea & Dessert Chains {#bubble-tea-desserts}

### Bubble Tea Tournament Recommendations

Challenge your family to try multiple chains and vote on favorites!

---

### Tiger Sugar (老虎堂)
**Famous For:** Brown sugar boba milk tea with signature "tiger stripes"
**Price:** $ (NT$80-120)
**Location:** No. 1, Nanyang Street, Zhongzheng District (and multiple locations)

**Why It's Famous:**
- Visually stunning drinks with caramelized brown sugar drizzled along cup walls
- Created the "brown sugar boba" trend
- Incredibly popular - long queues even in Taipei
- Perfect for Instagram photos (watch stripes slowly mix as you drink)

**What to Order:**
- Brown Sugar Boba Milk (signature drink)
- Brown Sugar Boba Milk with Cream Mousse

**Pro Tips:**
- Don't shake the drink immediately - take photos of stripes first!
- Drink through the stripes for best experience
- Queues can be 15-30 minutes - worth it
- Many copycat shops exist - look for the tiger logo

---

### Chun Shui Tang (春水堂)
**Famous For:** ORIGINAL inventor of bubble tea (since 1983)
**Price:** $ to $$ (NT$80-150)
**Locations:** Multiple in Taipei including:
- Shin Kong Mitsukoshi Taipei Station Store
- National Concert Hall
- Taipei Xinyi Place A9
- Nanxi Store One
- Dazhi

**Why It's Special:**
- The birthplace of bubble tea - literally invented it in Taichung in 1983
- Historic significance
- Authentic, traditional taste
- High quality ingredients
- Sit-down tea house atmosphere (not just takeaway)

**What to Order:**
- Pearl Milk Tea (the original!)
- Classic black tea with boba
- Snacks and light meals also available

**Pro Tips:**
- Can queue up to 1 hour at peak times
- Worth trying once for the history
- Comfortable seating - good for resting
- More expensive than chains but better quality

---

### 50 Lan (50嵐)
**Famous For:** Local favorite with great prices
**Price:** $ (NT$50-90)
**Locations:** EVERYWHERE in Taipei

**Why Locals Love It:**
- Consistently good quality
- Affordable prices
- Customizable sugar and ice levels
- No-frills but reliable

**What to Order:**
- Pearl Milk Tea
- Four Season Spring Tea
- Jasmine Green Tea with boba

- Specify sugar level: 0%, 30%, 50%, 70%, 100%
- Specify ice level: no ice, less ice, normal ice
- Ask for "qu bing" (less ice) to get more drink
- Most budget-friendly option

### Dietary Restrictions

#### Vegetarian/Vegan
- Say: "Wo chi su" (我吃素) = I eat vegetarian
- Taiwan has strong Buddhist vegetarian tradition
- Many restaurants have vegetarian options
- Look for "素食" signs
- Vegetarian restaurants exist throughout city
- Buddhist temple restaurants (cheap, vegetarian)

#### Halal
- Limited halal options in Taipei
- Research halal-certified restaurants in advance
- Fish and vegetarian options widely available
- Mosque near Taipei Main Station has list of halal restaurants

#### Gluten-Free
- Challenging in Taiwan (soy sauce contains wheat)
- Rice-based dishes are options
- Communicate clearly about allergy vs. preference
- Hot pot with rice noodles
- Grilled meats without marinades

#### Allergies
- Carry allergy cards in Chinese characters
- Peanut allergy: Taiwanese cuisine uses peanuts frequently
- Shellfish allergy: Common in Taiwanese food
- Be very clear about severity
- Hotels can often provide safer options

---

### Restaurant Etiquette & Tips

#### Ordering
- Point at pictures if language barrier exists
- "Zhe ge" (這個) = "This one"
- Many restaurants have photo menus
- Download Google Translate camera feature

#### Tipping
- NOT customary in Taiwan
- Don't tip at restaurants
- 10% service charge sometimes added at upscale restaurants (already included in bill)

#### Seating
- Wait to be seated at restaurants
- Shared tables common at casual spots
- It's normal to sit with strangers at small eateries

#### Table Manners
- Slurping noodles is acceptable
- Use tissue to cover mouth when using toothpick
- Chopsticks: Don't stick them upright in rice (funeral symbolism)
- Place chopsticks on rest or across bowl

#### Paying
- Usually pay at counter (not at table)
- Cash preferred at small restaurants
- Credit cards at larger establishments
- Split bills less common - easier to have one person pay

## Special Dining Experiences

### Night Market Guide

You'll visit three night markets on your trip. Here's how they compare:

**Raohe Night Market** (Day 1 Evening)
- Best for: Foodies, first-timers
- Size: Medium, one straight street
- Atmosphere: Authentic, local favorite
- Famous for: Pepper buns, variety of food
- Crowd level: Moderate
- Best time: 6:30-7:00 PM arrival

**Shilin Night Market** (Day 2 Evening)
- Best for: Entertainment, games, families
- Size: Largest in Taipei
- Atmosphere: Touristy but fun
- Famous for: XXL fried chicken, games area, underground food court
- Crowd level: Very crowded, especially weekends
- Best time: 6:00-7:00 PM arrival

**Ningxia Night Market** (Day 5 Evening)
- Best for: Authentic local experience, traditional food
- Size: Smallest, about 200 meters
- Atmosphere: Neighborhood vibe, mostly locals
- Famous for: Traditional Taiwanese snacks, lower prices
- Crowd level: Less crowded than others
- Best time: 6:30 PM arrival
