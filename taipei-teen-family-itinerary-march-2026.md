# Taipei Family Trip Itinerary - March 2026
## For Family with Teenagers (Ages 15 & 18)

---

## Weather & What to Pack for March

**Temperature Range:** 15-22°C (59-72°F), can drop to 10°C (50°F)
**Rainfall:** Expect around 16-20 rainy days with light drizzle (185mm total)
**Sunshine:** About 3 hours of bright sunshine daily

**Packing Essentials:**
- Layers (light sweaters, hoodies, long-sleeve shirts)
- Light rain jacket or umbrella
- Comfortable walking shoes (expect lots of walking!)
- Mix of summer and spring clothing
- Note: Few places have indoor heating, so even indoors can feel cool

**Special in March:** Cherry blossom season and Lantern Festival events!

## Day 1: Xinyi District - Modern Taipei

### Morning: Taipei 101 Observatory
**Location:** No. 7, Section 5, Xinyi Road, Xinyi District
**Time:** 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM
**Duration:** 2-3 hours

**Description:**
Start your trip at Taiwan's most iconic landmark! Taipei 101 was once the world's tallest building and features one of the fastest elevators globally (reaches 89th floor in just 37 seconds). The 89th floor observatory offers breathtaking 360-degree views of Taipei City.

**Why It's Perfect for Your Teens:**
- The engineering marvel of the massive golden Wind Damper (tuned mass damper) is fascinating for tech-curious teens
- Instagram-worthy photo opportunities with city panoramas
- The "Skyline 460" experience on the 101st floor includes an outdoor area for adventurous teens (ages 12+)
- Interactive exhibits including Star Wars and Doraemon displays
- The Cloud Forest Park exhibition features dreamy greenery installations perfect for photos

**Practical Details:**
- **Hours:** 9:00 AM - 10:00 PM (last entry 9:15 PM)
- **Price:** NT$600 for adults (around $20 USD), discounts for students
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours recommended
- **Book:** Pre-book tickets online to skip queues

**Pro Tips:**
- Visit right at opening (9 AM) to avoid crowds and get the best photos
- The elevator ride itself is an experience - watch the digital display showing the rapid ascent!
- Check if the "Beyond the Puppet, Beyond the Summit" exhibition is still running (scheduled until early March 2026)

### Afternoon: Elephant Mountain Hike
**Location:** Trailhead near Xiangshan MRT Station (just 0.85 km from Taipei 101)
**Time:** 2:30 PM - 5:00 PM
**Duration:** 2-3 hours total

**Description:**
Elephant Mountain (Xiangshan) is Taipei's most famous hike and Instagram hotspot. This short but steep trail rewards you with the best views of Taipei 101 against the city skyline. The mountain stands at only 183 meters high, making it achievable for most fitness levels.

**Why It's Perfect for Your Teens:**
- Active outdoor activity perfect for energetic teenagers
- The "Six Boulders" viewpoint is Taipei's most Instagrammable location
- Accomplishment factor - teens love the challenge of reaching the summit
- Only takes 20-30 minutes to reach the top, so not too demanding
- Natural jungle setting feels like an escape from the city
- Popular with local teenagers - great for people-watching and taking selfies

**Practical Details:**
- **Hours:** Accessible 24/7, but best at sunset or early morning
- **Price:** Free!
- **Difficulty:** Moderate - 0.7 miles with 446 ft elevation gain
- **Trail time:** 30-45 minutes up, 20-30 minutes down

**Reviews & Ratings:**
AllTrails rating of 4.5+ stars with reviewers praising the "stunning views" and noting it's "worth every step."

**Pro Tips:**
- Go on a weekday if possible - weekends get extremely crowded, especially at sunset
- Bring water bottles - you'll work up a sweat!
- The stairs are steep but well-maintained with handrails
- Best photo lighting is late afternoon (golden hour before sunset)
- There are multiple viewpoints along the trail, not just at the top
- Watch for humidity - March weather can make it feel warmer during the hike

### Evening: Raohe Night Market
**Location:** Raohe Street, Songshan District (near Songshan MRT Station)
**Time:** 6:30 PM - 9:00 PM
**Duration:** 2-3 hours

**Description:**
Raohe Night Market has emerged as Taipei's top night market for food lovers. It features one main 600-meter street lined with hundreds of food stalls, making it impossible to get lost. The straightforward layout is perfect for first-timers.

**Why It's Perfect for Your Teens:**
- Most serious foodies agree this has the best array of awesome foods in Taipei
- Carnival games scattered throughout for entertainment
- Manageable size (not overwhelming like Shilin)
- Great mix of traditional Taiwanese street food and trendy snacks
- Teens can safely explore on their own while you browse
- Perfect introduction to night market culture

**Practical Details:**
- **Hours:** 5:00 PM - 12:00 AM (best arrival time 6:30-7:00 PM)
- **Price:** Budget-friendly - NT$30-150 per food item
- **Getting there:** Songshan MRT Station (Exit 1 or 5)

**Must-Try Foods:**
- Pepper buns (胡椒餅) - Raohe's signature dish
- Fried oyster omelette
- Stinky tofu (challenge for brave teens!)
- Fresh fruit smoothies
- Grilled squid
- Bubble tea from various stands

**Reviews & Ratings:**
Consistently rated 4-4.5 stars with recent reviewers noting "incredible food variety" and "authentic local experience."

**Pro Tips:**
- Come hungry but pace yourself - there's a LOT to try
- Bring cash (some vendors don't accept cards)
- Look for stalls with long lines of locals - that's where the good food is!
- The entrance near the temple features a traditional arch - great photo op
- Try the pepper bun from the famous stall near the entrance (often has a long queue)

## Day 2: Youth Culture & Shopping District

### Morning/Afternoon: Ximending Shopping District
**Location:** Wanhua District (Ximen MRT Station)
**Time:** 10:00 AM - 5:00 PM
**Duration:** 5-7 hours (with lunch break)

**Description:**
Ximending is Taiwan's answer to Tokyo's Harajuku - a vibrant pedestrian-only district that's the epicenter of youth culture, entertainment, and trendy shopping. Streets are packed with fashion boutiques, pop culture shops, themed cafes, street performances, and endless photo opportunities.

**Why It's Perfect for Your Teens:**
- THIS IS THE PLACE FOR TEENAGERS! Attracts millions of young people annually
- Japanese kawaii culture meets Korean K-pop influences mixed with authentic Taiwanese creativity
- Tons of shops catering specifically to teen interests: fashion, sneakers, anime, manga, collectibles
- Claw machine arcades (hugely popular with local teens)
- Gachapon (capsule toy) shops everywhere
- Street performances, cosplay culture, and artistic expression
- Multiple movie theaters and KTV (karaoke) options
- Your teens will fit right in and potentially meet local teenagers

**Practical Details:**
- **Hours:** Most shops open 11:00 AM - 10:00 PM
- **Price:** Wide range - from affordable street fashion to mid-range brands
- **Getting there:** Ximen MRT Station (Exit 6 for main pedestrian area)

**Must-Visit Spots in Ximending:**

1. **Pop Mart Ximen Flagship Store**
   - Four-floor toy store paradise with exhibition space, VIP lounge, recording studio
   - Thousands of blind boxes and designer collectibles
   - Perfect for teens into pop culture and collecting

2. **Syntrend Creative Park (inside Ximending area)**
   - Not in Ximending proper but nearby - worth a taxi ride
   - 13-floor digital paradise with gaming, e-sports centers, VR experiences
   - Two floors dedicated to anime, manga, and collectibles
   - Tech-obsessed teens will love this

3. **Wannian Plaza**
   - Accessory shops and trendy boutiques
   - Street fashion at affordable prices

4. **FUFU Cat Cafe**
   - Unique experience: cafe with resident cats
   - Ages 12+ allowed
   - Relaxing break from shopping

5. **Cinema Street (Red House Area)**
   - Historic Red House building (great architecture photos)
   - Creative market on weekends
   - Hip independent shops and galleries

**Lunch Options in Ximending:**
- Modern Toilet Restaurant (quirky bathroom-themed restaurant - teens find it hilarious)
- Ay-Chung Flour-Rice Noodle (famous local noodles)
- Multiple bubble tea chains to try different flavors
- Japanese ramen shops
- Korean fried chicken restaurants

**Reviews & Ratings:**
Consistently rated as a must-visit for young travelers, with reviews highlighting "amazing energy," "great shopping," and "authentic youth culture experience."

**Pro Tips:**
- Let your teens have some independence here - it's very safe and easy to navigate
- Set a meeting point and time since phone signal can be spotty in crowded areas
- Bring extra cash - there are SO many tempting things to buy
- Check out the street performances in the main square (usually afternoons/evenings)
- Look for themed 7-11 stores - they're decorated specially for the district
- Take photos at the colorful murals and street art throughout the area
- Weekends are extremely crowded but have the best energy - weekdays are more relaxed

**Activity Break Ideas:**
- Try claw machines (budget NT$100-200 to test your skills)
- Get matching temporary tattoos (henna or stick-on types available)
- Visit a gachapon arcade and try your luck
- Stop at a maid cafe for a unique cultural experience
- Challenge the family to a KTV (karaoke) session

**Time:** 6:00 PM - 9:30 PM
**Duration:** 3-4 hours

**Description:**
Shilin is Taipei's largest and most famous night market, featuring hundreds of food stalls, extensive shopping areas, and the best games section of any night market in the city. It's a massive complex with both underground food court and outdoor street sections.

**Why It's Perfect for Your Teens:**
- BEST night market for families with older kids/teens
- Extensive games area - carnival-style challenges, basketball hoops, dart throwing, etc.
- Teens love the competitive games and chances to win prizes
- Massive variety means everyone finds something they like
- More entertainment-focused than other night markets
- Great balance of food, shopping, and activities
- Popular with local teenagers, so great atmosphere

**Practical Details:**
- **Hours:** 4:00 PM - 12:00 AM (peak time 7:00-10:00 PM)
- **Price:** Budget-friendly - games NT$50-100, food NT$40-200
- **Getting there:** Jiantan MRT Station (Exit 1), 5-minute walk
- **Layout:** Underground food court + outdoor street stalls

**Must-Try Foods:**
- XXL fried chicken cutlet (bigger than your face!)
- Oyster pancakes
- Hot star fried chicken
- Assorted tempura
- Fried buns (shengjian bao)
- Sweet potato balls
- Various fruit juices

**Must-Do Activities:**
- Games area (basketball, ring toss, water gun games)
- 3D Tiger Cub photo spot
- Shopping for trendy accessories and clothing
- Try to win stuffed animals at claw machines

**Reviews & Ratings:**
4.0-4.5 stars on TripAdvisor and Google with 20,000+ reviews. Visitors praise "amazing atmosphere," "great for families," and "fun games area."

**Pro Tips:**
- Arrive around 6-7 PM before peak crowds
- Underground food court is air-conditioned (nice if it's drizzling)
- Set a game budget for teens beforehand or they'll spend forever there!
- The outdoor section has the best atmosphere but gets crowded
- Look for the famous hot star fried chicken stall (always has queues)
- Bring a small bag for shopping finds
- Watch your belongings in crowded areas

## Day 3: Cultural Day & Nature

### Morning: National Palace Museum
**Location:** No. 221, Section 2, Zhishan Road, Shilin District
**Time:** 9:00 AM - 12:30 PM
**Duration:** 3-4 hours

**Description:**
The National Palace Museum houses the world's largest collection of Chinese imperial artifacts and artworks - nearly 700,000 pieces spanning 8,000 years of history. Originally from Beijing's Forbidden City, these treasures were relocated to Taiwan in 1949. The museum's architecture itself is stunning, set against mountain scenery.

**Why It's Perfect for Your Teens:**
- The "Three Treasures" are genuinely fascinating and spark curiosity
- **Jadeite Cabbage** - World-famous jade carving that looks incredibly realistic
- **Meat-Shaped Stone** - Natural stone that looks exactly like braised pork belly (teens find this hilarious and mind-blowing)
- **Mao Gong Ding** - Ancient bronze vessel with historical significance
- Interactive exhibits on Level 2 with videos and virtual tours make it engaging for younger visitors
- Not just "boring old stuff" - the craftsmanship is genuinely impressive
- Great for teens interested in history, art, or Asian culture
- The quirky nature of some exhibits (like the meat stone!) makes it memorable

**Practical Details:**
- **Hours:** 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM (closed Mondays)
- **Price:** NT$350 for adults, NT$150 for students (bring student ID!)
- **Duration:** Minimum 2 hours, ideally 3-4 hours
- **Audio Guide:** NT$150 rental (available in English) - highly recommended!
- **Getting there:** Take MRT to Shilin Station, then bus R30 or taxi

**What to See:**
- **Level 3:** Jade collection (including the famous Jadeite Cabbage), bronzes, weapons, ritual vessels, Ming and Qing carvings
- **Level 2:** Interactive area, painting and calligraphy galleries, rotating special exhibitions
- **Level 1:** Ceramics collection

**Reviews & Ratings:**
4.0-4.5 stars on TripAdvisor with 8,000+ reviews. Teens specifically mention the "cool treasures," "meat stone is awesome," and "better than expected."

**Pro Tips:**
- Arrive right at opening (9 AM) on a weekday to avoid tour groups
- Rent the audio guide - it brings the exhibits to life with interesting stories
- Download the NPM app beforehand for additional information
- Start on Level 3 where the most famous items are located
- The museum cafe has good views and is a nice break halfway through
- Make it a game: challenge teens to find the Three Treasures and take photos
- Photography is allowed in most areas (no flash)
- The gift shop has unique souvenirs - the "meat stone" merchandise is popular

### Afternoon: Beitou Hot Springs
**Location:** Beitou District (Xinbeitou MRT Station)
**Time:** 2:30 PM - 6:00 PM
**Duration:** 3-4 hours

**Description:**
Beitou is Taipei's hot spring capital, located in a mountain valley just 20 minutes from central Taipei via MRT. The area features natural hot springs, Japanese-era bathhouses, museums, and scenic parks. It's the perfect place to relax after days of sightseeing, especially in March's cooler weather.

**Why It's Perfect for Your Teens:**
- Unique cultural experience most Western teens haven't tried
- Hot springs are naturally relaxing after hiking and walking
- March weather is ideal for hot springs (cool outside, warm water)
- Spring City Resort has family-friendly outdoor pools where you wear swimsuits (unlike traditional nude hot springs)
- Mix of relaxation and exploration - not just sitting in water
- Beautiful natural setting with mountain views
- Free foot baths are fun and low-commitment introduction
- Teens find the volcanic activity and thermal vents fascinating

**Practical Details:**
- **Getting there:** Take MRT Tamsui-Xinyi Line to Beitou, transfer to Xinbeitou Line (1 stop)
- **Price Range:**
  - Free foot baths in parks
  - Public hot springs: NT$40-50
  - Spring City Resort day pass: NT$800-1,200 per person
- **What to bring:** Swimsuits (for family-friendly venues), towels, water bottles

**Activities in Beitou:**

1. **Beitou Thermal Valley (地熱谷)**
   - FREE attraction
   - Stunning turquoise hot spring lake with steam rising
   - Temperatures reach 80-100°C (don't touch the water!)
   - Great photo opportunity
   - Educational signs explain volcanic activity
   - 15-minute walk from Xinbeitou MRT

2. **Beitou Hot Spring Museum**
   - FREE admission
   - Housed in beautiful Japanese-era bathhouse (built 1913)
   - Learn about hot spring culture and history
   - Tatami rooms and historic architecture
   - Must remove shoes inside (wear clean socks!)

3. **Fuxing Park Foot Bath**
   - FREE public foot bath
   - Casual, fun experience
   - Great for testing if teens like hot springs
   - 5-minute walk from Xinbeitou MRT

4. **Spring City Resort (recommended for families)**
   - FAMILY-FRIENDLY with swimsuits required
   - Outdoor pools including cool pool for swimming
   - Multiple temperature pools
   - Shallow pools and deeper pools
   - Day pass available (don't need to stay overnight)
   - Book in advance, especially on weekends

**Alternative Options:**
- **Beitou Outdoor Public Hot Springs:** NT$40-50, swimsuits required, budget option
- **Private hot spring room at a hotel:** More privacy, higher cost (NT$1,500-3,000 for 60-90 minutes)

**Reviews & Ratings:**
Beitou consistently rated 4.0-4.5 stars. Families note "great experience," "relaxing," "teens enjoyed it more than expected."

**Pro Tips:**
- Visit the free attractions (Thermal Valley, Museum, foot bath) first to build excitement
- If doing private hot springs, book ahead - March is busy
- Spring City Resort fills up on weekends - reserve online in advance
- Bring your own towels to save rental fees
- Stay hydrated - hot springs can be dehydrating
- Don't stay in hot water more than 15-20 minutes at a time
- The area has nice restaurants for dinner afterward
- Combine with Yangmingshan National Park if you have extra time and weather is good

## Day 4: Day Trip - Jiufen, Shifen & Yehliu

**Location:** Northeast Taiwan Coast
**Time:** 8:00 AM - 7:00 PM (full day)
**Duration:** 10-11 hours

**Description:**
One of Taiwan's most popular day trips combines three spectacular destinations: Yehliu's alien-like rock formations, Shifen's sky lantern traditions and waterfall, and Jiufen's nostalgic mountain village. This trip offers diverse experiences from geological wonders to cultural traditions to foodie adventures.

**Why It's Perfect for Your Teens:**
- **Yehliu Geopark:** Mind-blowing rock formations shaped by erosion - looks like another planet. Perfect for unique photos and geology nerds.
- **Shifen:** Releasing sky lanterns is magical and Instagram-worthy. Write wishes, watch them float away. Plus waterfall hiking.
- **Jiufen:** Allegedly inspired Studio Ghibli's "Spirited Away" - anime fans go crazy for this. Narrow streets, red lanterns, traditional teahouses.
- Mix of outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, and photo opportunities
- Everything is highly shareable on social media
- Not just sightseeing - interactive experiences (lantern release, hiking)
- Manageable amounts of walking between activities
- Great balance for a full day

**Practical Details:**
**Option 1: Guided Tour (Recommended)**
- **Price:** NT$1,000-1,500 per person via Klook or KKday
- **Includes:** Transportation, English-speaking guide, entrance fees
- **Duration:** 9-10 hours
- **Departure:** Taipei Main Station (usually 8:00-8:30 AM)
- **Pros:** No planning needed, efficient routing, knowledgeable guide
- **Cons:** Less flexibility, set schedule

**Option 2: Independent Travel**
- **Pros:** More flexibility, potentially cheaper
- **Cons:** Complex logistics, more time consuming
- **Not recommended for first-timers** - the routes are complicated

**What to Expect:**

### Stop 1: Yehliu Geopark (野柳地質公園)
**Time:** 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM (90 minutes)

**Description:**
Stunning geological formations along the coast created by erosion. The most famous is the "Queen's Head" rock formation that looks like an Egyptian queen's profile. The entire cape is covered in honeycomb, mushroom, and ginger-shaped rocks.

**Why Teens Will Love It:**
- Otherworldly landscape perfect for creative photos
- Science meets art - fascinating geology
- Feels like exploring an alien planet
- Unique backdrop unlike anything back home

**Pro Tips:**
- The Queen's Head is currently fragile - viewing distance enforced
- Wear good walking shoes - surfaces are uneven
- Windy on the coast - bring a light jacket
- Follow marked paths - safety is important near cliff edges

---

### Stop 2: Shifen Old Street & Waterfall (十分)
**Time:** 12:00 PM - 3:00 PM (3 hours including lunch)

**Description:**
Charming old street where the railway tracks run right through the middle of town. The main attraction is releasing sky lanterns with written wishes. Shifen Waterfall, often called "Taiwan's Niagara," is a 15-minute walk away.

**Why Teens Will Love It:**
- **Sky Lantern Release:** Write wishes and messages with calligraphy brushes, choose lantern colors (each color represents different wishes), then release it into the sky. It's meaningful, fun, and makes amazing photos/videos.
- **Railway Tracks:** Trains pass directly through the street - you literally stand on tracks for photos (when trains aren't coming!)
- **Waterfall:** Beautiful 20-meter cascade with rainbow mist on sunny days. Short hike with scenic views.
- **Snacks & Shopping:** Quirky shops and traditional treats

**Practical Details:**
- **Sky Lantern Cost:** NT$150-200 depending on colors (1-4 colors)
- **Lunch:** Local restaurants along old street (NT$100-250 per person)

**Must-Do:**
- Release at least one family lantern together
- Let each teen release their own lantern too
- Take photos on the railway tracks
- Hike to the waterfall (20-30 minute round trip)
- Try traditional peanut ice cream rolls

**Pro Tips:**
- Lantern vendors will take photos/videos of your release with your phone - tip not required but appreciated
- Trains come through regularly - vendors will shout when to clear the tracks
- Best photos of lanterns are with others in the background for scale
- The waterfall hike is easy - totally worth it
- Try the "mountain pig sausage" from street vendors

---

### Stop 3: Jiufen Old Street (九份老街)
**Time:** 4:00 PM - 6:30 PM (2.5 hours)

**Description:**
A historic gold mining village perched on a mountain overlooking the ocean. Narrow cobblestone streets wind up the hillside, lined with red lanterns, traditional teahouses, souvenir shops, and food stalls. At dusk, the lanterns illuminate and create magical atmosphere.

**Why Teens Will Love It:**
- **Spirited Away Connection:** Architecture and lanterns resemble the Studio Ghibli film (though studio hasn't confirmed). Anime fans recognize the vibe immediately.
- **Aesthetic Overload:** Every corner is photo-worthy - red lanterns, mountain views, ocean backdrop, traditional architecture
- **Food Paradise:** Called "the foodie capital of Taiwan" - endless snacks and treats
- **Teahouse Experience:** Traditional tea service overlooking the ocean - surprisingly relaxing for teens
- **Unique Shopping:** Handmade crafts, quirky souvenirs, traditional items

**Must-Try Foods:**
- Taro balls (芋圓) - Jiufen's signature dessert
- Fish balls
- Peanut mochi
- Sweet potato cakes
- Grilled squid
- Herbal meatballs

**Activities:**
- Explore the narrow alleyways (A-Mei Teahouse area is most famous)
- Visit a traditional teahouse for tea and views (30-60 minutes, NT$200-400 per person)
- Shop for unique souvenirs
- Watch the sunset from viewpoints
- Take LOTS of photos once lanterns are lit

**Reviews & Ratings:**
Jiufen + Shifen day trips consistently rated 4.5-5 stars. Recent reviews mention "highlight of Taiwan trip," "teens loved the lanterns," "magical experience."

**Pro Tips:**
- **Timing is key:** Arrive in Jiufen late afternoon to see both daylight and dusk lantern lighting
- **Crowds:** Weekends are PACKED. Tours usually visit on weekdays when possible.
- **Hills & Stairs:** Jiufen is very hilly with many stairs - wear comfortable shoes
- **Weather:** Check forecast - the area gets foggy/rainy. Mountain mist can be atmospheric or obstructive depending on thickness.
- **Teahouses:** Reserve a window seat if possible for best views
- **Navigation:** Easy to get lost in alleyways - that's part of the fun! But set a meeting point.
- **Shopping:** Prices are higher than Taipei but items are unique to the area

**Tour Company Recommendations:**
- **Klook:** "Yehliu, Jiufen, Shifen and Golden Waterfall Day Tour" - highly rated
- **KKday:** Similar offerings with good reviews
- **Book online in advance** - tours fill up, especially in March (nice weather month)

## Day 5: Tech, Gaming & Entertainment

### Morning/Early Afternoon: Syntrend Creative Park (三創生活園區)
**Location:** No. 3, Section 1, Civic Boulevard, Zhongzheng District (near Taipei Main Station)
**Time:** 10:00 AM - 2:00 PM
**Duration:** 3-4 hours

**Description:**
Syntrend Creative Park is a 13-floor digital paradise and technology mall that's become a mecca for gaming, anime, and tech enthusiasts. This isn't just a shopping mall - it's an entertainment destination with e-sports arenas, VR centers, gaming cafes, pop-ups, and two entire floors dedicated to anime, manga, and collectibles.

**Why It's Perfect for Your Teens:**
- **Tech Heaven:** Latest gadgets, gaming equipment, computers, and electronics
- **Gaming Culture:** Multiple e-sports centers where you can watch competitions or participate
- **VR Experiences:** Several VR centers offering games and experiences
- **Anime & Manga:** Floors 11-12 are dedicated to Japanese pop culture - figurines, manga, collectibles, merchandise
- **Hands-On:** This isn't window shopping - you can try games, test VR, experience technology
- **Youth Culture Hub:** Frequented by local teens and young adults with similar interests
- **Air Conditioned:** Perfect backup plan if March weather is rainy

**Practical Details:**
- **Hours:** 11:00 AM - 10:00 PM
- **Price:** Browsing is free; VR experiences NT$100-400, gaming cafe NT$50-150/hour
- **Getting there:** 5-minute walk from Taipei Main Station (Exit M8)
- **Nearby:** Ximending is walking distance if you want to return there

**What to Do:**
1. **Gaming Cafes:** Rent stations to play latest PC games (Overwatch, League of Legends, etc.)
2. **VR Centers:** Try VR gaming experiences, escape rooms, simulations
3. **E-Sports Viewing:** Watch competitive gaming if tournaments are happening
4. **Anime Floors:** Browse massive collectible selection, figurines, manga
5. **Tech Shopping:** Check out latest phones, cameras, accessories
6. **Arcade:** Rhythm games, fighting games, claw machines
7. **Cafes:** Gaming-themed cafes and restaurants throughout

**Reviews & Ratings:**
4.0-4.5 stars with reviewers noting "perfect for gamers," "teens loved it," "could spend all day here."

**Pro Tips:**
- Teens might want to split up and explore different floors based on interests
- Set a budget for VR experiences beforehand
- Bring phone chargers - lots of photo opportunities
- Some gaming cafes require ID (passport works)
- Check if any special events or tournaments are scheduled during your visit
- The building is massive - easy to spend 3-4 hours here
- Lunch options available inside (multiple restaurants and food court)

### Afternoon: Huashan 1914 Creative Park (華山1914文化創意產業園區)
**Location:** No. 1, Section 1, Bade Road, Zhongzheng District
**Time:** 2:30 PM - 5:30 PM
**Duration:** 2-3 hours

**Description:**
A former wine factory transformed into Taipei's coolest arts and culture space. This sprawling creative park features art galleries, pop-up exhibitions, indie shops, design stores, vintage markets, cafes, and outdoor spaces. The industrial architecture mixed with modern art creates an incredibly photogenic environment.

**Why It's Perfect for Your Teens:**
- **Constantly Changing:** Always something new happening - exhibitions rotate frequently
- **Photo Paradise:** Industrial-meets-modern aesthetic is Instagram gold
- **Youth Culture:** Attracts creative young people, artists, and designers
- **Variety:** Art exhibits, design stores, vintage shopping, cafes - something for everyone
- **Chill Vibe:** More relaxed than shopping districts, good for winding down
- **Cultural Experience:** See Taiwan's contemporary creative scene
- **Free to Enter:** Only pay for specific exhibitions you want to see

**Practical Details:**
- **Hours:** 9:30 AM - 9:00 PM (hours vary by individual shops/exhibitions)
- **Price:** Park grounds are FREE; special exhibitions NT$100-400
- **Getting there:** 5-minute walk from Zhongxiao Xinsheng MRT Station (Exit 1)

**What to Do:**
1. **Explore Grounds:** Wander the outdoor spaces, old factory buildings, art installations
2. **Check Exhibitions:** See what temporary exhibits are running (photography, design, pop culture)
3. **Vintage Market:** Weekend markets often have vintage clothing, accessories, crafts
4. **Design Shops:** Browse indie design stores for unique souvenirs
5. **Cafes:** Multiple trendy cafes - good for bubble tea or snack break
6. **Photo Session:** Industrial architecture, murals, art pieces everywhere
7. **Live Events:** Sometimes live music, performances, or workshops

**Similar Alternative:** Songshan Cultural and Creative Park (松山文創園區) - same concept, different location. If you have time, visit both, or choose based on proximity to your next activity.

**Pro Tips:**

## Essential Information & Tips

### Transportation

**MRT (Metro) System:**
- Incredibly efficient, clean, and easy to use
- English signage throughout
- Get an **EasyCard** (rechargeable transit card) at any MRT station - NT$500 (NT$100 deposit + NT$400 credit)
- Can use EasyCard for MRT, buses, convenience stores, some taxis
- Trains run 6:00 AM - midnight
- Download **Metro Taipei** app for route planning

**Taxis:**
- Safe and relatively affordable
- Use **TaxiGo** app (English-friendly, like Uber)
- Yellow taxis widely available
- Always metered - insist on meter or use app
- Most drivers don't speak English - have address in Chinese characters ready

**Walking:**
- Taipei is very walkable, especially central districts
- Sidewalks can be narrow - watch for scooters parked on them
- Traffic moves fast - cross at marked crosswalks

### Money & Budgeting

**Currency:** Taiwan Dollar (NT$ or TWD)
**Exchange Rate:** Approximately NT$30 = $1 USD (check current rates)

**Budget Estimates (per person per day):**
- **Food:** NT$600-1,000 ($20-33) - mix of street food and restaurants
- **Transportation:** NT$200-400 ($7-13) with MRT/buses
- **Attractions:** NT$300-1,000 ($10-33) - varies by activity
- **Shopping/Entertainment:** Variable based on preferences
- **Total:** NT$1,500-3,000 ($50-100) per person per day excluding accommodation

**Money Tips:**
- **Cash is King:** Many night markets and small shops are cash-only
- **ATMs:** 7-Eleven ATMs accept international cards with good rates
- **Credit Cards:** Accepted at larger stores, hotels, restaurants - less common at small vendors
- **Bring Cash:** Have NT$2,000-3,000 per person at all times for flexibility

### Cultural Etiquette

**Do's:**
- Remove shoes when entering homes, some restaurants, temples
- Use both hands when giving/receiving items (polite)
- Queue orderly - Taiwanese respect lines
- Be quiet on public transportation
- Try to learn basic Mandarin phrases - appreciated!

**Don'ts:**
- No eating or drinking on MRT (seriously enforced - NT$1,500-7,500 fine)
- Don't stick chopsticks upright in rice (funeral symbolism)
- Avoid loud talking in public spaces
- Don't tip - not customary in Taiwan
- No smoking in most public areas

**Basic Mandarin Phrases:**

- How much: Duōshǎo qián (多少錢)
- Delicious: Hǎo chī (好吃)
- Excuse me: Bùhǎo yìsi (不好意思)
- I don't eat meat: Wǒ chī sù (我吃素)

## FAQ for Taipei with Teens

**Q: Is Taipei safe for teenagers?**
A: Extremely safe. It's one of Asia's safest cities with very low crime rates. Teens can have reasonable independence in tourist areas.

**Q: Do people speak English?**
A: In tourist areas and among younger people, yes. Older generations less so. MRT and major attractions have English signage. Translation apps help.

**Q: What if my teens are picky eaters?**
A: Taiwan has Western options (McDonald's, KFC, Starbucks), plus familiar Asian foods like fried rice, noodles, and fried chicken. Convenience stores have packaged snacks.

**Q: Can we drink tap water?**
A: Taiwanese drink filtered water. Hotels provide filtered water. Convenience stores sell bottled water cheaply. Tap water is technically safe but locals don't drink it.

**Q: Is March a good time to visit?**
A: Yes! One of the best months - weather is mild, not too hot, less rain than summer. Cherry blossom season. Avoid major crowds.

**Q: How much cash should we carry daily?**
A: NT$2,000-3,000 per person ($65-100 USD) for daily expenses. Night markets and small shops are cash-only.

**Q: Will my teens get bored?**
A: Unlikely with this itinerary! Taipei has diverse activities from tech/gaming to nature to food to shopping. There's something for every interest.

**Q: Can vegetarians eat well in Taipei?**
A: Yes! Taiwan has a strong Buddhist vegetarian tradition. Many restaurants have vegetarian options. Say "wo chi su" (I eat vegetarian).

**Q: Should we book tours in advance?**
A: For the Jiufen/Shifen day trip, yes - books up especially in nice weather months. Other attractions can be done walk-in, but pre-booking saves time.

**Q: What if weather is bad?**
A: This itinerary has indoor backup options: museums, creative parks, shopping malls, themed cafes. March rain is usually light drizzle, not storms.
